The Problem

Requirements Documentation Is Broken

Every enterprise team feels it. The process that should drive delivery is the one that slows it down the most.

⏳

40-60 Hours Per Sprint

Teams spend entire sprint cycles manually writing, reviewing, and iterating on epic specifications. That's engineering capacity burned on documentation instead of delivery.

⚠

Inconsistent Quality

Every team writes differently. Every product owner has their own format. The result? Scope creep, ambiguous requirements, rework, and missed deadlines — every single sprint.

🔄

Context Switching Tax

Write in Confluence. Manage issues in GitLab. Track sprints in another tool. Copy-paste between them. The workflow is fragmented and every switch costs cognitive load.

Invisible Sprint Status

Scrum Masters spend standup time chasing updates. "What's the status?" is the most asked question in every retro. Status should be generated, not gathered.

The Engine

A 6-Stage AI Pipeline
That Thinks Before It Writes

Not a chatbot. Not a template filler. FRAME runs your input through six distinct AI stages — each with a specific purpose, calibrated temperature, and quality gate.

1

Comprehension

AI reads your rough input and extracts entities, requirements, gaps, risks, and dependencies. It understands what you meant — even when you didn't say it all.

AI-Powered Analysis
2

Classification

Three-vote consensus assigns your epic to one of 10 enterprise categories — API, Frontend, Data Pipeline, Security, Infrastructure, and more. Each category unlocks domain-specific templates and quality criteria.

3-Vote Consensus
3

Structural Analysis

Every section of your epic is scored 1-10 on completeness, clarity, and specificity. The AI generates a transformation plan — a surgical roadmap for what needs to be rewritten, expanded, or restructured.

Section-Level Scoring
4

Intelligent Refinement

Per-section rewriting powered by iterative feedback loops. Each section is refined independently, then cross-validated for coherence. Contradictions are caught. Cross-references are aligned. Nothing falls through the cracks.

Iterative Feedback Loop
5

Structured Output

Auto-generated architecture diagrams (flowcharts, sequence diagrams, state machines) and user stories with full acceptance criteria. Your epic arrives with visual documentation that stakeholders can immediately understand.

Diagrams + User Stories
6

Validation Gate

Quality metrics. Requirement traceability. Pass/fail gate. The pipeline loops Stages 4-6 until the document meets the quality threshold — or reports exactly where it falls short. Nothing ships unless it passes.

Quality Gate

Your 2-paragraph idea becomes a 15-section enterprise specification — with architecture diagrams, user stories, acceptance criteria, and requirement traceability — fully validated before anyone reviews it.
